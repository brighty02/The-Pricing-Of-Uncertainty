# The Pricing Of Uncertainty

A first-principles exploration of uncertainty, information, confidence, and the formation of prices.

Overview

This repository contains Pricing Uncertainty, an original research essay exploring a different way of thinking about markets.

Rather than treating markets primarily as mechanisms for discovering prices, the essay argues that markets are better understood as systems for transferring uncertainty. Under this framework, prices emerge as a consequence of a deeper sequence:

Information → Beliefs → Confidence → Action → Price

The essay develops this idea progressively, introducing a conceptual framework for how rational agents interpret uncertainty before markets have fully priced it. It also explores how information propagates through interconnected networks and why this perspective may become increasingly relevant in a future containing trillions of markets.

Status

This essay is an early-stage research note.

Its purpose is to introduce a conceptual framework rather than present a finished theory. Many of the mathematical models introduced here are intentionally simple and are expected to evolve as the research develops.

Future work may include:

* A more rigorous mathematical treatment of confidence formation.
* Graph-based models of information propagation across markets.
* Extensions to prediction markets and market creation.
* Empirical testing and simulation of the proposed framework.

Inspiration

This work was inspired by Grant Stenger’s essay Trillions of Markets.

While Trillions of Markets explores how markets emerge as the cost of market creation approaches zero, Pricing Uncertainty asks a complementary question:

How should a rational agent value uncertainty before a market has discovered its price?
